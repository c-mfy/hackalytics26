about speechwrite

speech right
with speech
write

speechwrite is a research tool that measures how fairly automatic speech recognition systems treat speakers of different English accents — and makes the global picture visible. we believe voice technology should work for everyone, not just those who sound like the training data.

the problem

accent bias in AI speech

state-of-the-art speech-to-text systems are trained predominantly on North American and British English audio. speakers with African, South Asian, East Asian, or Caribbean accents face word error rates two to five times higher than their counterparts — making voice interfaces effectively less functional for hundreds of millions of people.

this is not a fringe issue. it affects healthcare dictation, courtroom transcription, automated customer service, and accessibility tools that people depend on daily.

how it works

the pipeline

1
audio upload
you upload a speech recording in any common format — mp3, wav, m4a, ogg, or flac. recordings can be from yourself, participants, or sourced from public datasets like mozilla common voice or the dialect archive.
2
speech-to-text transcription
we pass the audio through one or more STT models — openai whisper, google speech-to-text, or azure cognitive services. you choose the model to test.
3
WER computation
we compare the machine transcript against your reference ground truth using word error rate — the standard metric for speech recognition accuracy, counting substitutions, deletions, and insertions.
4
globe visualization
your result is plotted on the globe alongside aggregated data from all other tested regions, creating a living heatmap of STT equity across the world.
the metric

word error rate

WER is the standard benchmark for measuring STT accuracy. it compares a machine transcript to a ground truth reference and counts three types of errors:

WER = (Substitutions + Deletions + Insertions) / N
where N = total words in the reference transcript
0 – 15%
low bias
15 – 30%
medium bias
30 – 60%+
high bias
